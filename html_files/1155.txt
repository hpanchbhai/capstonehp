## Advisory Board News

The Accounting Advisory Board has added 3 new members, please help welcome
James Lynch, Kim Mc Cormick and Jeff Nishita. The Advisory Board is a group of
business leaders and accounting professionals who provide advice to the
Accounting Department to better prepare our students for career challenges.

The Accounting Advisory Board has added 3 new members. Please help welcome
James Lynch, Kim McCormick and Jeff Nishita. A complete list of the 2017-2018
Advisory Board can be found
[here.](https://www.scu.edu/business/accounting/about/advisory-board/)

![Jim Lynch](/media/leavey-school-of-business/accounting/actg-
news/NewBoard2.jpg)

**James Lynch**

James Lynch returns as a member of the Accounting Advisory Board in Fall 2017.
Jim received his bachelor’s degree from SCU in 1983.  He has been the CFO and
Treasurer of SJW Corp since 2010, responsible for all facets of accounting,
tax, treasury, and investor relations functions of the corporation.  Before
joining SJW Group, Jim was an audit partner of KPMG for fourteen years. At
KPMG, he was responsible for managing Board and “C” level business
relationships with companies in KPMG’s Consumer Markets and Information,
Communications, and Entertainment Practices. Currently, Jim is the chairman of
California Water Association Board Finance and Accounting Committee as well as
an Executive Committee Board member of Silicon Valley Chamber of Commerce.  He
was the president of the SCU Accounting Advisory Board for many years.

* * *



![Kim McCormick](/media/leavey-school-of-business/accounting/actg-
news/NewBoard1.jpg)

**Kim McCormick**

Kim brings 30 years of experience working with all types of public sector
organizations including higher education, cultural arts and museums,
international relief organizations, environmental advocacy organizations,
private foundations, cities, transit districts, employee benefit plans, school
districts and other FASB and GASB-reporting entities, many of which require an
annual audit under Uniform Guidance. Kim’s attention is fully dedicated to
serving the public sector and she leads the Greater Bay Area office’s public
sector and not-for-profit industry group. Kim has experience working both as a
member of the Firm’s National Professional Standards Group and as a line audit
partner giving her both a technical/ quality focus and with significant
practical, in- the- trenches experience conducting audits.

* * *



![Jeff Nishita](/media/leavey-school-of-business/accounting/actg-
news/NewBoard3.jpg)

**  Jeff Nishita**

Jeff Nishita is a partner in the San Francisco office of Novogradac & Company
LLP. Mr. Nishita provides various services in the real estate industry,
including audit and tax services, consulting, forecasting, LIHTC and tax
exempt bond application assistance and training. He is experienced with HUD,
A-133, and various state audit requirements. In addition, he is in charge of
maintaining the firm’s Financial Forecast Model. He works with for-profit and
nonprofit developers, syndicators and direct investors. He is the founder of
the firm’s LIHTC Working Group and community service program and has served on
the Housing Colorado NOW! Conference Committee since 2013. Before joining
Novogradac, Mr. Nishita worked with start-up companies and small businesses in
executive positions. He received an M.B.A. with honors from Santa Clara
University and a degree in economics concentrating in accounting from UC Santa
Barbara. He is licensed as a C.P.A. in California and Colorado.
