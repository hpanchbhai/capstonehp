flowing, the following list of resources is helpful for providing you some of
the resources you need for a successful business.

##### Business Plans

__

It is vital for all startups to begin planning with a clear business model and
purposeful strategy. Check out the following links for additional help with
business planning:



  * [Sample 1 Page Executive Summary](/media/leavey-school-of-business/cie/Sample-Executive-Summary.doc)-A concise, easy to read format
  * Bplans.com - Your business starts here.  
[www.bplans.com](http://www.bplans.com/)

  * Deming Center for Entrepreneurship - [Writing a Successful Business Plan](http://leeds-faculty.colorado.edu/moyes/html/documents/WritingaSuccessfulBusinessPlan2009.pdf)
  * Biztools - Build a better business  
[www.biztools.com](http://www.biztools.com/)

  * Free [Legal Documents](https://www.upcounsel.com/Public/GuidesAndDocuments?t=Documents) Courtesy of UpCounsel
  * Free [Legal Guides](https://www.upcounsel.com/Public/GuidesAndDocuments?t=Guides) Courtesy of UpCounsel
  * Guy Kawasaki's [The Only 10 Slides You Need in a Pitch](http://guykawasaki.com/the-only-10-slides-you-need-in-your-pitch/)



##### Organizations

__

There are various local and national networks to join as a small business,
which may may help you with a variety of valuable resources, a strong support
group, and much more. Take a look below for networks that may interest you.

  * U.S. Small Business Administration  
[www.sba.gov](http://www.sba.gov/)

  * SCORE - Counselors to America's Small Business  
[www.score.org/](http://www.score.org/)

  * Silicon Valley SCORE  
<http://www.svscore.org/>

  * U.S. Chamber of Commerce Small Business Nation  
[www.uschambersmallbusinessnation.com](http://www.uschambersmallbusinessnation.com/)

  * National Federation of Independent Businesses  
[www.nfib.com](http://www.nfib.com/)

  * National Association for the Self-Employed  
[nase.org](http://www.nase.org/)

##### Silicon Valley SBA-Friendly Banks

__

Following is a sampling of just a few area banks that make SBA-backed loans,
including contact information (the links below are links to the SBA loan area
of each institution):

  * Superior Financial Group in Walnut Creek  
(925) 296-0500, Jim Bek

  * [Pinnacle Bank](https://pinnacle.bank/business/) in Morgan Hill  
(408) 762-7163, Barbara Holland or Mark Ruiz

  * [Wells Fargo](https://www.wellsfargo.com/biz/) (while larger, it does do smaller SBA guaranteed loans)  
(408) 995-3660, Greg Mitchell

  * [U.S. Bank NA](http://www.usbank.com/cgi_w/cfm/small_business/products_and_services/financing/sba_loans_ps.cfm)  
(800) 303-4722

In addition, many credit unions offer SBA-backed loans, such as Tech CU in the
Silicon Valley area. Gaebler.com has a [list of links by
city](http://www.gaebler.com/business-loan-sources-for-cities-in-CA) for banks
with business loans, most of which offer SBA loans.
