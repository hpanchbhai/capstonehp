Management majors are those who want to develop balanced general management
skills or to specialize in human resource management.



![](/media/leavey-school-of-business/academics/aerial-android-wallpaper-
architectural-design-373912-360x200.jpg)

> Over 807,000 new jobs in management occupations are expected to be added to
the U.S. economy between 2016 to 2026.

- [U.S. Bureau of Labor Stastics](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/management/)



## Potential Management Careers

A broad range of employment and career opportunities are open to Management
majors. Feedback from recent alumni identifies the following as possible
directions:

  * Hotel management
  * Human resource specialist in both retail and high tech organizations
  * Management consulting with major consulting firms
  * Retail management
  * Self-employment
  * Graduate study, such as law school
  * Conference planning
  * Management role in family business
  * Sales management

**Career Resources**

**[Undergraduate Career Center](/careercenter/ "Home » Home » Career
Center")**  
Use the Career Center as a hub for employment resources. Among their offerings
are: job listings, resume critiques, mock interviews, and even same-day
advising appointments.

**[Graduate Business Career Management](/business/graduates/career-management/
"Home » Home » Leavey School of Business » Current Students » Career
Management")**  
GBCM offers consulting services, skill development programs, recruiting
events, and other resources for graduate students to facilitate successful
management of one's career and/or job search.
